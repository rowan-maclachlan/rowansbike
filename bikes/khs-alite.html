The Origin

Rowan got this bike... for his 16th birthday? And he's still using it!

The Upgrades

Less has been done on this that on Rowan's Cambio Rino, but there have been a few major changes. The first of these was the replacement of the broken grip shifters (shifter prone to wearing out) with a pair of used trigger shifters found at the Bridge City Bike Coop. This alone represented a huge upgrade. The next was the replacement of the front shocks. The stock shocks were a cheap make, and couldn't be services. After several years of rough riding as a boy and the accompanying disinterest in its proper maintenance, the shocks had becomd quite siezed and unreponsive. One day, I was walking past the church on the corner of Eastlake and 12th Street East when I noticed a pair of front shocks sitting on a table that said "Free Stuff". I could hardly believe it! Upon closer inspection, Rowan discovered that he had simply happened upon a pair of lightly used RockShox Toras. Although the Toras are one of RockShox lower tiers, they represented a huge upgrade over the practically unusable shocks which were on the KHS at the time.

The installation of these RockShox, though, would lead to much more work that I had originally planed. The KHS came with rim brakes, but the new fork had no mounts for the cantilever brakes the bike came with. Instead, it had a mount for a disk brake. About $175 later, I retrieved a new wheel, new tire, and new disk brake and disk brake mount adapter from Bike Universe on 20th. These, combined with the new fork, significantly upgrades the overall quality of the ride while at the same time expanding my experience with front suspension, disk brakes, and this type of upgrade generally.
