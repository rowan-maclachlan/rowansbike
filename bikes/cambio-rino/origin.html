I found this bike in his neighbour's garage, sometime in the Spring or Summer of 2016. I had just had my other bike stolen - a heavy Norco 21-speed commuter with shocks, flat bars, trigger shifters, and a big cushy seat. How it was stolen is a story for another time, though.

This bike was in bad condition when I found it. Although it was stored inside a garage when I got it, it had obviously been left out in the elements at too many point during its life. It had some cool camo bartape which was flayed and falling apart - covering up badly rusted and ugly little steel drop bars. Its center-pull brakes were not rusted away, but they were covered in dirt and grime. The brake levers featured some clunky and uninspiring skull-brakes. At the time, I didn't even know these existed. The tires were the only non-original part on the bike when I got it - cheap 27x1 1/4" Pneu Vee tires with the vomit-tan sidewalls. The rims were cheap garbage: they were out of true when I got it, but I was never able to get them to stay true. In hindsight, I think they may have just needed a complete rebuilding. Even though this period was the beginning of my education as a mechanic, I have never before seen rims of such poor quality. They felt as though they could have been cut in half with a butter knife. The seat and seatpost, too, were cheap. They had the old style of seat clamp, and the post was a mere 6" deep. It was thoroughly rusted into the seat-tube, and it took ages and the greater part of a can of WD-40 to get it loose. It had a couple of cheap stem-shifters with some broken friction adjusters, too. In many ways, its a small miracle the frame has been repurposed with such care and attention as it was.
