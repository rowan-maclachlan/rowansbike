The Cambio Rino

The Origin

Rowan found his bike in his neighbour's garage, sometime in the Spring or Summer of 2016. He had just had his other bike stolen - a decent but heavy Norco 21-speed commuter with shocks, flat bars, trigger shifters, and a big cushy seat. How it was stolen is a story for another time, though.

This bike was in bad condition when Rowan found it. Although it was inside a garage when he got it, it had obviously been left out in the elements at too many point during its life. It had some cool 'camo' bartape which was flaying and falling apart - covering up badly rusted and ugly little steel drop bars. Its center-pull brakes were not rusted away, but they were covered in dirt and grime. The brake levers featured some clunky and uninspiring skull-brakes. At the time, I didn't even know these abominations existed. The tires were the only non-original part on the bike when I got it - cheap 27x1 1/4" Pneu Vee tires with the vomit-tan sidewalls. The rims and hubs were cheap garbage: they were out of true when I got it, but I was never able to get them to stay true. Even though this period was the beginning of my education as a mechanic, I have never before seen rims of such poor quality. They felt as though they could have been cut in half with a butter knife. The seat and seatpost, too, were cheap. They had the old style of seat clamp, and the post was a mere 6" deep. It was incredible rusted into the seat-tube, and it took ages and the greater part of a can of WD-40 to get it loose. It had a couple of cheap stem-shifters with some broken friction adjusters, too. In many ways, its a small miracle the frame has been repurposed with such care and attention as it was.

The Upgrades

Despite its humble beginnings, the bike has gone through significant transformations. On Sunday May 12th, 2019, I had the mechanics at the Bike Doctor in Saskatoon replace the last the original component on the bike - the fascinating Shimano Selecta bottom bracket and crankset - with a used Shimano Tiagra Hollowtech II triple crankset.
